Our Solution

GPS-first architecture that keeps riders informed when things go wrong.

Right now, PRT's system trusts scheduled data first and patches in GPS as a secondary check. PRT Live flips that. Vehicle position is the source of truth. When a bus goes off-route, it stays on the map with a clear status.

Three Pillars of PRT Live

Automated Trip Association

Automatically match buses to their scheduled runs using vehicle ID, time, block assignment, and GPS position with no manual login required.

  • Removes the head-sign failure mode
  • Reduces gaps during trip transitions

Automatic Off-Route Detection

When a bus goes off-route, flag it, keep it on the map, pause the ETA until things are clear, and alert the team automatically.

  • Stops map disappearances
  • Turns a reactive process proactive

Transparent Communication Layer

Give riders honest ETAs that are tagged with confidence levels and push detour banners to both the PRT app and third-party apps.

  • Improves rider decision quality
  • Closes the gap between first and third-party apps

Current Experience vs. PRT Live

Current State

  • Schedule-first logic can mask real-world drift
  • Unexpected detours can drop buses from public tracking
  • Riders can't tell if a bus is late, gone, or just unconfirmed
  • Detour visibility varies by platform and pay tier

PRT Live State

  • GPS-first tracking keeps the map grounded in reality
  • Off-route buses stay visible with clear status tags
  • Confidence states communicate uncertainty honestly
  • Better detour feeds mean fairer access across all apps

Strategic Analysis

Business Value

Lower complaint volume and clearer reliability data give the agency better tools for internal decision-making.

Value Chain Fit

Targets the exact failure points: inbound data linking, detour handling during operations, and outbound rider communication.

Capital-Light Feasibility

Uses the existing Clever Devices and HASTUS stack. No new hardware — just better integration and workflow design.

Social Theory

Sociotechnical Systems Theory

You can't just fix the software — the people and processes around it need to work together too. PRT Live redesigns the handoffs, not just the code.

Technology Acceptance Model

People use tools they trust. Showing honest ETAs — even uncertain ones — builds more credibility than silently hiding the problem.

Affordance Theory

When riders know what's actually happening, they can act on it — wait, reroute, or switch modes. Ambiguity just causes frustration.

Expected Impact

Organizational

Better reliability data and fewer complaint tickets — because riders will actually know what's going on.

Societal

People who depend on transit most get more consistent, honest information about their daily commute.

Risks & Trade-offs

Operators may push back on automation, third-party apps need integration updates, and rolling this out requires buy-in across multiple teams.
