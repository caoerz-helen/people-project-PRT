Our Solution

Right now, PRT's system trusts scheduled data first and patches in GPS as a secondary check. PRT Live solves this by using vehicle location as the source of truth. So, when a bus goes off-route, it stays on the map with a clear status.

Three Pillars of PRT Live

Automated Trip Association

Automatically match buses to their scheduled runs using vehicle ID, time, block assignment, and GPS position so that no manual login by drivers is required.

  • Removes the head-sign failure mode where drivers just set the head sign and never log in, leaving the bus completely off the map.
  • Eliminates those short, confusing periods where a bus vanishes from the tracker between consecutive trips.

Automatic Off-Route Detection

When a bus goes off-route, flag it, keep it on the map, pause the ETA until things are clear, and alert PRT's disruption management team and dispatchers automatically.

  • Stops map disappearances
  • Turns a reactive process proactive

Transparent Communication Layer

Give riders honest ETAs that are tagged with confidence levels and push detour banners to both the PRT app and third-party apps.

  • Improves rider decision quality
  • Closes the gap between first and third-party apps

Current Experience vs. PRT Live

Current State

  1. Sticking to a schedule manually controlled by drivers can where a bus really is
  2. Unexpected detours can drop buses from public tracking
  3. Riders can't tell if a bus is late, gone, or just unconfirmed
  4. Detour visibility varies by platform and pay tier, so key information is hidden from riders who cannot afford a paid subscription

PRT Live State

  1. GPS-first tracking keeps the map grounded in reality
  2. Off-route buses stay visible with clear status tags
  3. Confidence states communicate uncertainty to riders
  4. Better detour feeds mean fairer access across all apps since riders no longer need a paid subscription to see detour information

Strategic Analysis

Business Value

Cleaner data and fewer complaints help PRT see real problems and fix them with confidence.

Value Chain Fit

Targets the exact current failure points of inbound data linking, detour handling during operations, and outbound rider communication.

Low Cost Feasibility

Uses the existing software stack of Clever Devices and HASTUS stack, so No new hardware will be involved, just better integration and workflow design.

Social Theories Behind PRT Live

Sociotechnical Systems Theory

You can't just fix the software, the people and processes around it need to work together too. PRT Live redesigns those processes and not just the code.

Technology Acceptance Model

People use tools they trust, so showing honest ETAs (even if they are uncertain ones) builds more credibility than silently hiding the problem.

Affordance Theory

When riders know what's actually happening, they can act on it by waiting, rerouting, or switching modes. The current ambiguity just causes frustration.

Expected Impact

Organizational

With better reliability data, complaint tickets become fewer and more streamlined. Riders will understand what's happening, and because the app is run by PRT (not third parties), complaints go directly to the agency.

Societal

People who depend on transit most get more consistent, honest information about their daily commute.

Risks & Trade-offs

Operators may push back on automation, third-party apps need integration updates, and rolling this out requires buy-in across multiple teams.
