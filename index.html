PRT Live

Rebuilding rider trust in Pittsburgh Regional Transit

PRT already has the GPS hardware and network infrastructure it needs. The problem is what happens when buses go off-route, tracking breaks down, and riders are left with no information on their bus location. PRT Live is built to fill in this gap.

See Our Solution

Live Detour Context

Keep vehicles visible during disruptions instead of dropping them from public tracking.

The Problem

Ghost Bus Experience

Buses that are physically running can disappear from apps when they go off-route or lose their trip association, which is the data link between the bus's physical location and its planned route.

Silent Failure States

There's no way to tell if a bus is running late, actually gone, or just dropped off the tracking system.

Equity Pressure

People who rely on transit the most are hit hardest when the information is unreliable.

Where things break down

PRT already has GPS hardware, BusTime predictions, and app integrations. However, the chain breaks down in three specific spots.

01

Trip Association Gaps

How an operator logs in to their bus's on board computer system determines whether a bus appears on the map. One method links the bus to its route automatically; another method requires drivers to loggin in trip‑by‑trip, which creates gaps in a buses location and what a rider can see on their app. A third method commonly used involves setting only the head sign which makes the bus completely invisible to riders, even though GPS is working.

02

Off-Route Dropouts/Ghost Buses

Unexpected detours can pull buses out of public view until a dispatcher manually enters the detour in the system.

03

Fragmented Rider Visibility

Detour information shows up differently depending on whether you're using the PRT app or a third-party app.

"It's always tracking, but it depends what it's being, what it is able to link to show to the public."

Scott Vetere, Director of Transportation Technology at PRT, explaining why a running bus can still be invisible to riders.

"It would just be the manual process of putting those detours in those day-to-day incidents..."

Scott Vetere, Director of Transportation Technology at PRT, on why unplanned detours show ghost buses to riders
